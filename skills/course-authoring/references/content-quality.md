# Content Quality

## Platform intent

This repo is trying to make high-quality contribution easier, not lower the bar. Agents should accelerate writing and structure, but the resulting material still needs to feel deliberate, teachable, and role-aware.

## Tone

- clear over clever
- direct over academic
- supportive over performative
- practical over vague

## Depth by level

### Conceptual

Aim for:

- intuition first
- concise explanations
- useful analogies
- small exercises that force the learner to think

Avoid:

- implementation-heavy tangents too early
- unexplained jargon
- long theory dumps

### Builder

Aim for:

- concrete workflows
- implementation trade-offs
- evaluation and review loops
- artifacts a team could actually produce

Avoid:

- generic "best practices" with no operating context
- cargo-cult architecture advice
- lists of tools without decision criteria

## Role awareness

Engineering content should emphasize systems, reliability, observability, and implementation boundaries.

Product content should emphasize use-case selection, UX, measurement, rollout, and cross-functional trade-offs.

Business content should emphasize workflow leverage, operating models, governance, ROI logic, and adoption.

## Checklist

Before finishing, ask:

- Is the learner audience obvious from the first screen?
- Does the chapter teach one real idea instead of many half-ideas?
- Would a motivated learner know what to do next after reading it?
- Is the level appropriate for conceptual vs builder?
- Are the examples relevant to the target profession?
