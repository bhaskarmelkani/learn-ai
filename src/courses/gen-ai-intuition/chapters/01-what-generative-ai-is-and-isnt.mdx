# What Generative AI Is and Isn't

Generative AI is software that produces **new content** by extending patterns it learned from large amounts of data.

That content can be:

- text
- images
- audio
- video
- code
- structured drafts like summaries, outlines, or extracted fields

The key word is **generate**.

A generative system does not just look up a stored answer. It creates a response token by token, pixel by pixel, or frame by frame based on patterns it learned before you arrived.

## Four Useful Distinctions

| Tool pattern | Main job | Example output |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Search | Find existing information | links, passages, sources |
| Predictive AI | Choose a label or number | spam/not spam, fraud score |
| Rules-based software | Follow fixed instructions | "if status is late, send reminder" |
| Generative AI | Create a new artifact | a draft, answer, image, or plan |

Generative AI can be combined with the other three, but it is not identical to any of them.

That matters because people often expect the wrong thing:

- they expect a chatbot to be a search engine
- they expect a creative model to behave like a calculator
- they expect a workflow assistant to know private company facts without being given them

<Callout type="idea" title="First durable habit">
When you see an AI product, ask: is it mainly generating, retrieving, classifying, or following rules? Many product mistakes come from confusing those jobs.
</Callout>

## What Generative AI Is Good At

Generative systems are often strong at:

- drafting
- rewriting
- summarizing
- extracting structure from messy text
- translating between formats
- brainstorming variations

They are often weaker at:

- guaranteeing truth
- knowing your private documents without being given them
- making high-stakes decisions without review
- handling ambiguous instructions that humans think are "obvious"
