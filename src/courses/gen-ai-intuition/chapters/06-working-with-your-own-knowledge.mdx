One of the biggest beginner misconceptions is:

> "The model is smart, so it probably knows our company information too."

Usually, it does not.

If the answer depends on:

- your handbook
- your pricing page
- your notes
- your CRM records
- your support docs

then the workflow usually needs **grounding**.

Grounding means giving the system relevant context from a trusted source so it can answer with better evidence.

## What Retrieval Adds

Retrieval answers a simple question first:

> Which source or chunk of source material should the model see before it responds?

That is the heart of many document Q&A systems.

The term **RAG** usually means retrieval-augmented generation:

1. retrieve relevant context
2. pass that context into the prompt
3. generate an answer from that grounded context

You do not need infrastructure detail to get the beginner intuition. The important distinction is:

- pure generation guesses from prior patterns
- grounded generation answers with selected evidence in context
