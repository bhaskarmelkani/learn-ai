Generative AI often feels smart because it is very good at producing:

- fluent language
- plausible structure
- smooth transitions
- familiar reasoning patterns

Those are real capabilities.

But they do **not** prove:

- factual accuracy
- access to the right evidence
- stable judgment under ambiguity
- awareness of your private context

## Hallucination Is a Workflow Problem, Not Just a Buzzword

A hallucination is a confident-looking output that is unsupported, invented, or unjustified.

That can happen because:

- the prompt was underspecified
- the model lacked the needed facts
- the context was noisy or contradictory
- the workflow asked the model to sound complete even when evidence was weak

The healthiest beginner habit is not "assume the model lies." It is:

> Ask what evidence this answer was actually based on.

## Three Common Beginner Mistakes

1. trusting style as proof
2. skipping verification on familiar-looking topics
3. asking the model to guess where a lookup or source check would be better
