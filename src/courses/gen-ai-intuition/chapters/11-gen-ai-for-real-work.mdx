The most durable Gen AI advantage is not one flashy prompt.

It is a small set of workflow patterns you can reuse:

- draft from rough notes
- summarize for a specific audience
- extract structure from messy text
- compare options against criteria
- brainstorm variations
- turn long material into a next-step plan

## Five Reliable Work Patterns

| Pattern | Good for | Core review question |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Drafting | emails, first-pass memos, outlines | did it preserve the real facts and tone? |
| Summarizing | meetings, reports, articles | what important nuance was lost? |
| Extraction | action items, fields, claims, risks | did it miss or invent any key item? |
| Comparison | vendors, plans, approaches | are the criteria explicit and evidence-based? |
| Planning | project next steps, study plans, workflows | are the steps realistic and properly prioritized? |

A useful beginner upgrade is to stop asking:

> "What can Gen AI do?"

and start asking:

> "Which workflow pattern am I in, and what check does that pattern need?"
