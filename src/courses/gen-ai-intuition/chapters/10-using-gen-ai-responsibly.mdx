Responsible use is not mainly about sounding ethical.

It is about noticing where a workflow could:

- expose sensitive information
- mislead someone with false certainty
- produce harmful or deceptive media
- create a result that looks authoritative without the right expertise

## Four Questions to Ask Before You Use Gen AI

1. does this involve sensitive or private data?
2. could a wrong answer cause meaningful harm?
3. does this output need expert sign-off?
4. could this artifact mislead someone if taken at face value?

If several answers are yes, slow down the workflow and add more review.

## Common Responsibility Zones

| Risk area | Why it matters | Safer habit |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Privacy | prompts may include sensitive or internal information | share the minimum needed, use approved tools |
| Copyright and provenance | outputs may draw on unclear source boundaries or generate misleading claims of originality | review reuse carefully, avoid blind publishing |
| Deepfakes and synthetic media | realistic-looking media can mislead or impersonate | label synthetic content where needed, verify source media |
| High-stakes advice | errors in medical, legal, financial, or HR contexts can harm people | require expert review and original sources |

<Callout type="warning" title="A good red flag">
If the output will be trusted by someone who cannot easily verify it, your review bar should go up immediately.
</Callout>
