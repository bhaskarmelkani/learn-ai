One score can create one boundary.

That is powerful, but it has a clear limit: some decision problems do not look like one clean split.

Imagine applicants who should be approved if they have either:

- very strong income
- or very strong savings

That creates two good regions, not one simple dividing line.

<DecisionBoundaryBridgeDemo />

## The Missing Idea

If one boundary is too simple, we need more than one useful signal.

Instead of asking the model for one final score immediately, we can first ask it to build smaller internal signals like:

- "does this look like high income?"
- "does this look like strong savings?"
- "does this look like low risk?"

Those intermediate signals are often called **features**, **detectors**, or **representations**.

## Why This Matters

This is the real bridge to neural networks.

Neural networks are not powerful because they are mysterious. They are powerful because they can learn several useful internal signals and then combine them.
