The same house-price story that made lines feel natural also shows where lines break.

For smaller and mid-sized homes, price may rise steadily. But premium homes can rise faster because location, scarcity, and luxury effects start to matter more.

That creates a bend.

## The Need Comes First

Before naming anything, just look at the problem:

- the trend is still upward
- but the shape is no longer straight
- so one line now feels too rigid

That means we need a model with more shape.

<NonlinearDemo />

## Naming the Idea

Once we allow the model to bend, we are in the world of **non-linear models**.

One simple version is a curved equation like:

$$y = ax^2 + bx + c$$

That specific family is often called **polynomial regression**.

## Same Data, Different Shapes

The important intuition is not the name. It is seeing how different model shapes behave on the same data.

## The Bigger Lesson

A line is great when the world looks roughly linear.

But if the pattern bends, the model needs more expressive shape. That is the first hint that simple models may not be enough for all problems.
