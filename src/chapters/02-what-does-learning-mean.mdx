# What Does Learning Mean?

Suppose we want a model that helps estimate house prices.

At first, the model's guesses will be rough. The whole point of learning is to make those guesses better.

That is all training really is:

1. make a prediction
2. compare it with reality
3. adjust the model's numbers
4. try again

## The Intuition

When people first hear "machine learning," it can sound mysterious.

But the core idea is ordinary:

- start with a model shape
- show it examples
- nudge its numbers until its predictions improve

<Callout type="analogy">
Think of tuning a guitar. The guitar shape stays the same, but you keep adjusting the tuning pegs until the sound gets better. Training does the same thing with parameters.
</Callout>

## Two Common Kinds of Prediction

The predictions we will study in this course mostly fall into two families:

| Problem | Output | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Predict a number | continuous value | house price |
| Make a decision | category | approve / reject |

The names for those families come later. First we want to feel the need for them.

## A Concrete Question

Imagine we know these two house sales:

| Size (sqft) | Price |
|-------------|-------|
| 1000 | $190k |
| 2000 | $290k |

Now a third house appears at 1500 sqft.

What is the most obvious first way to guess its price?

Most people do the same thing: they imagine a line between the known examples and place the new house somewhere along that trend.

That is where we will start next.

<Exercise title="Prediction First">
Before going to the next chapter, make a quick guess for the 1500 sqft house. You do not need a formula yet. Just use the trend you see.
</Exercise>

<Checkpoint prompt="What changes during learning: the examples, or the model's numbers?">
The examples stay fixed. The model's numbers change so its predictions line up better with those examples.
</Checkpoint>

<Callout type="idea" title="Takeaway">
Learning is not magic. It is repeated improvement: predict, compare, adjust, repeat.
</Callout>
