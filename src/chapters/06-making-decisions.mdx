So far the model predicted numbers.

Now imagine a different kind of question:

- approve or reject a mortgage
- spam or not spam
- cat, dog, or bird

These are not number-prediction problems. They are **decision** problems.

## Start with a Score

A model can still begin by producing one raw score.

For example:

- low score -> reject
- high score -> approve

The simplest first decision rule is a **threshold**.

<ClassificationLab />

## Smooth the Decision

Hard thresholds are useful, but probabilities are easier to reason about than raw scores.

So we take the raw score and squash it into the 0-to-1 range.

That squashing curve is called **sigmoid**, and the resulting model is called **logistic regression**.

<SigmoidDemo />

## More Than Two Classes

Now imagine the model must choose between several answers instead of just yes or no.

The same idea extends: turn several raw scores into a probability distribution.

That function is called **softmax**.
